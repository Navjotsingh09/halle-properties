7 Ways Landlords Can Refresh Their Property - the Kids Are Back at School

2 minutes read 26 January 2026 Kam Kulair

The summer holidays are always full-on. Between family commitment, days out and keeping the children entertained, there's often little time to focus on property matters. Now that the kids are back at school and routines have returned, landlords finally have the breathing space to give their rental properties the attention they deserve.

This is the perfect moment to carry out improvements that can boost rental income, keep tenants happy, or even prepare your property for sale. With calmer schedules and clearer priorities, landlords can confidently plan essential seasonal upgrades. Here are seven practical steps to consider this season.

1. Catch Up on Repairs

Minor issues are easy to overlook during busy months, but tenants and buyers notice them quickly. Fix dripping taps, squeaky doors, cracked tiles or flaking paintwork. A well-maintained home shows care and instantly adds appeal.

2. Book a Deep Clean

After months of use, properties benefit greatly from a professional clean. Fresh carpets, polished windows and spotless kitchens or bathrooms create a brighter, more welcoming space. First impressions matter.

3. Improve Energy Efficiency

With colder months ahead, tenants will be thinking about energy bills. Simple upgrades such as draught-proofing, LED lighting or smart heating controls can make a property cheaper to run and more attractive.

Enhance Safety, Appeal and Market Readiness

4. Refresh Outdoor Space

Gardens, driveways and communal areas can easily fall behind over summer. Clearing leaves, trimming hedges and tidying outdoor spaces now, will keep the property looking well cared for and improve kerb appeal before the colder months set in.

5. Check Safety Measures

Peace of mind matters. Take time to test smoke alarms, carbon monoxide detectors and window or door locks. These simple checks reassure tenants and buyers that safety is a priority.

6. Update Your Marketing

If you are planning to let or sell soon, think about how your property is presented online. Autumn light is perfect for photography, so updating your images and marketing materials now ensures your listing looks its best.

Conclusion

7. Get Expert Advice

You do not have to manage everything on your own. Partnering with a local property expert can save time, reduce stress and help you make the right decisions about letting or selling.
