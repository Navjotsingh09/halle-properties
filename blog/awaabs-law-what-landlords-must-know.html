Awaab's Law: What Landlords Must Know About Damp, Mould & Legal Responsibility in 2025

Author: Kam Kulair
Landlord Tips & Legal Compliance
Read Time: 2 Minutes

The introduction of Awaab's Law is a defining moment for housing standards in the UK; it's not just for social landlords. At Halle Properties, we're urging private landlords to prepare now, as the government signals its intention to extend these new legal obligations to the private rented sector.

As these changes move closer to becoming law for private landlords, it's important to understand what will be expected. Awaab's Law introduces strict timelines for investigating and resolving hazards, meaning delayed responses will no longer be acceptable.

Introduction

Awaab's Law, named after two‑year‑old Awaab Ishak, was introduced after his tragic death caused by prolonged exposure to mould in a social housing flat. The law sets strict deadlines for investigating and resolving damp and mould complaints, ensuring that landlords take immediate action. This shift highlights the government's commitment to tenant safety and prevents hazardous conditions from being ignored or delayed.

Although currently applied to social housing under the Social Housing (Regulation) Act 2023, similar rules are expected to be extended to private landlords through the upcoming Renter's Rights Bill. Once implemented, private landlords will be legally required to respond quickly and thoroughly to complaints.

This change reinforces the importance of proactive property management, protecting tenants' health while safeguarding the long‑term condition of rental homes.

Under Awaab's Law, landlords will be required to:

  • Investigate complaints within 14 days
  • Begin repairs within 7 days of identifying a hazard
  • Complete all necessary work within 21 days

These deadlines are not just best practice; they will be enforceable. Landlords who fail to act could face legal action, fines, or even criminal liability if tenant health is compromised.

This shift has prompted many landlords to reassess their current maintenance processes. Questions around liability, contractor delays, and tenant reporting timelines are becoming more urgent. At Halle Properties, we take a proactive approach by conducting regular inspections, document all tenant communications and work with reliable contractors who can respond quickly.

It's also important to understand that this law is not just about compliance, it's about protecting tenant health and maintaining the long-term value of your property. With over 2.7 million households in England affected by damp or mould, the government is taking a firm stance on landlord accountability.

As the private rented sector becomes more regulated, Halle Properties is here to support landlords with expert guidance, trusted maintenance partners, and up-to-date legal insights. Awaab's Law is coming, and preparation is key.

Awaab's Law represents a major shift in how damp and mould are treated within the UK rental sector, placing clear and enforceable responsibilities on landlords. As the legislation moves toward inclusion of the private rented sector, early preparation is essential.
