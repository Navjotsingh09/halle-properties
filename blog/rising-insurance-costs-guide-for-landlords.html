Navigating Rising Insurance Costs: A Guide for Landlords in 2025

Author: Kam Kulair
Insurance Costs & Property Management
Read Time: 2 Minutes

As we move through 2025, landlords across the UK are seeing a significant rise in insurance premiums due to inflation, rising construction costs, and increased claim frequencies. At Halle Properties, we believe it's essential for landlords to understand these changes and take proactive steps to reduce their impact.

At Halle Properties, we believe landlords can manage rising insurance pressures by acting early. Simple steps such as regular maintenance, accurate rebuilding valuations, and yearly policy reviews help reduce risks and control premium increases.

Understanding the Surge in Insurance Premiums

After two decades of relatively stable property insurance rates, premiums are now rising to reflect the changing risk landscape. Inflation, measured by the Consumer Price Index (CPI), has reached levels not seen in over a decade.

At the same time, the cost of building materials has increased by over 20% due to supply chain issues and higher demand. These factors have driven up repair and rebuilding costs, prompting insurers to adjust premiums accordingly.

Properties that are older, located in higher-risk areas, or lack modern safety features may face steeper increases as insurers factor in the likelihood of future claims.

Other Factors Driving Premium Increases

Several additional elements are contributing to higher insurance costs:

  • More Frequent and Costly Claims: Insurers are seeing an increase in both the number and cost of claims.
  • Climate Change and Extreme Weather: Events such as floods and storms are becoming more common, increasing risk levels.
  • Labour Shortages: A shortage of skilled construction workers is increasing repair costs, which contributes to higher premiums.

Rising insurance premiums are a challenge for landlords, but understanding the reasons behind the increases and taking proactive steps can help reduce their impact.

Strategies to Manage Rising Insurance Costs

Halle Properties recommends the following practical steps to help landlords navigate the rising cost of insurance:

  • Review and Update Policies: Regularly check that your insurance provides sufficient cover for today's higher repair and rebuild costs.
  • Invest in Property Maintenance: Well-maintained properties are less likely to lead to claims and demonstrate to insurers that your property is professionally managed.
  • Consider a Higher Excess: Opting for a higher policy excess may reduce premiums, but ensure it's an amount you could comfortably pay if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are landlord insurance premiums rising?

Insurance premiums are rising due to inflation, increased building material costs (up over 20%), more frequent claims, climate-related events like floods and storms, and labour shortages in the construction industry.

How can I reduce my insurance premiums as a landlord?

You can reduce premiums by maintaining your property well, reviewing and updating policies regularly, considering a higher excess, and ensuring accurate rebuilding valuations.

Should I review my insurance policy annually?

Yes, annual reviews are essential. They ensure your cover is adequate for current rebuilding costs and help you find competitive rates in a changing market.

Does property location affect insurance costs?

Yes, properties in higher-risk areas (flood zones, areas with high crime rates) typically face higher premiums as insurers factor in the likelihood of claims.
