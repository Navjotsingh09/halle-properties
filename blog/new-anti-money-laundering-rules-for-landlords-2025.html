New Anti-Money Laundering Rules for Landlords: What You Need to Know in 2025

2 minutes read 1 December 2025 Kam Kulair

The UK lettings sector is undergoing another major shift, with stricter anti–money laundering (AML) rules now affecting everyday landlords. This means landlords must understand how AML checks work, what information they must collect, and the penalties for non‑compliance.

At Halle, we're here to break it down and make the process simple. Our team helps landlords understand their responsibilities, complete the right checks, and stay fully compliant without added stress. With the latest regulations now applying across the board, having expert guidance ensures you protect your business with confidence.

What Landlords Need to Do Under the New Rules

Landlords Now in Scope
AML rules now apply to all landlords, regardless of rent or property type.

Mandatory Sanctions Checks
Both tenants and landlords must be checked against government sanctions lists for new tenancies and annually for ongoing ones.

Let-Only Arrangements & Agents
If you use an agent for tenant placement or rent collection, clarify who handles AML compliance.

Potential Costs & Proof
AML checks may incur fees, and platforms like Rightmove or Zoopla may request compliance proof before listing.

Final Thoughts

These new AML rules mark a significant shift in how lettings are regulated. While the changes may feel complex, they're designed to protect the integrity of the rental market and reduce financial crime.
