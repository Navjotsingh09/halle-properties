Creative Smart Decoration Ideas for Renters in the UK

Author: Kam Kulair
Renter-Friendly Decorating Tips
Read Time: 2 Minutes

Decorating a rented home in the UK can feel limiting, but there are plenty of creative and smart ways to personalise your space without risking your deposit. Removable wallpaper, peel‑and‑stick tiles, temporary hooks, and modular furniture allow you to transform a room while keeping everything reversible. Soft furnishings like rugs, cushions, throws, and curtains are also powerful tools for adding personality and warmth.

However, renters must still be aware of the boundaries. Most landlords prohibit permanent alterations such as repainting walls, drilling holes, or replacing fixtures without written permission. Structural changes, built‑in shelving, or altering plumbing or electrics are also typically not allowed. Before starting any decorative project, renters should review their tenancy agreement and when needed, request approval.

Renter-Friendly Decorating: Ideas That Don't Need Landlord Approval

1. Temporary Wall Art – Use frames with picture hooks or removable strips to hang art without damaging walls.

2. Removable Wallpaper & Decals – Peel-and-stick options add style without permanent changes.

3. Rugs & Floor Coverings – Stylish rugs can transform a space; use non-slip mats for safety.

4. Reversible Changes – Small swaps like shower curtains or cupboard knobs add impact and are easy to undo.

5. Soft Furnishings – Cushions, throws, and curtains add colour and texture without permanent alteration.

What You Can't Do Without Landlord's Approval

Certain alterations or improvements to the property generally require permission from your landlord. Without proper approval, these changes could result in deductions from your deposit or even lead to eviction in extreme cases.

1. Painting Walls: While it's tempting to personalise your home with a fresh coat of paint, most landlords prohibit tenants from changing the wall colours.

2. Installing Permanent Fixtures: Fitting shelves, curtain rails, or any other fixtures that require drilling into walls typically requires permission.

3. Renovations and Structural Changes: Altering the layout of your property or making structural changes is strictly prohibited unless your landlord gives explicit consent.

4. Gardening and Landscaping: If you have access to an outdoor space, make sure to ask your landlord about any restrictions on gardening.

Tenant Support: Your Rights and Legal Advice

Renters in the UK have a number of rights to ensure that they live in safe, comfortable, and well-maintained properties. As a tenant, you should be aware of the legal protections that are in place to support you.

1. Check Tenants' Right to Rent: It's essential to verify your right to rent in the UK, especially if you're a non-EU national. Landlords are legally required to carry out a "Right to Rent" check before you can move into a property.

2. Protection of Tenancy Deposits: By law, your landlord must protect your deposit in a government-approved tenancy deposit scheme (TDP).

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I hang pictures in my rental without permission?

Yes, you can use removable adhesive strips or picture hooks that don't require drilling. These cause minimal damage and are easy to remove when you move out.

Is removable wallpaper safe for rental properties?

Generally yes, peel-and-stick wallpaper is designed to be removable without damaging walls. However, always test a small area first and check your tenancy agreement.

What happens if I make changes without approval?

Unapproved changes could result in deductions from your deposit to cover restoration costs, or in serious cases, could be grounds for eviction.

Can I paint the walls if I repaint them back?

Even with good intentions, painting without permission is risky. Always get written approval from your landlord first, as mismatched colours could still affect your deposit.
